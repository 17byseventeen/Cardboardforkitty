About Us

Meet the team behind Cardboard for Kitty

Grace Totten

Co-Founder

Grace Totten is the co-founder of Cardboard for Kitty, an initiative that blends creativity, compassion, and sustainability to support the well-being of cats through recycled design. What began as a love for animals has grown over time as Grace has come to realize that, with each year she gets older, the issues she cares about extend far beyond cats to include environmental responsibility, community action, and global impact.

She is also the founder of 17 by Seventeen, a youth-led initiative focused on taking local action toward achieving the 17 United Nations Sustainable Development Goals. Through her work, Grace hopes to show that small, creative ideas can help address much bigger challenges.

Grace's sister Hannah, who co-founded Cardboard for Kitty with her, has since left the event in Grace's capable hands, allowing her to continue growing this community-driven initiative.

Our Mission

Cardboard for Kitty brings together creativity, sustainability, and community giving in a unique annual event. We believe that small, creative actions can create meaningful change, supporting both animal welfare and community well-being while promoting environmental responsibility.

Through our partnership with RedHead Strays and First Steps Saint John, we're able to extend our impact to help both paws and people in our community.
