17 by Seventeen Initiative

In 2026, Cardboard for Kitty is proud to be part of the 17 by Seventeen initiative, a community-driven movement aligned with the United Nations' 17 Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs). This initiative connects local community efforts with global sustainability objectives, emphasizing partnerships, responsible consumption, climate action, and community well-being. By participating in 17 by Seventeen, we're joining a network of organizations committed to creating positive, sustainable change in our communities while contributing to broader global goals.

Our Story

Cardboard for Kitty is a community fundraiser founded by sisters Hannah and Grace Totten in 2018. What began as a creative idea to help local animals has grown into an annual event that brings together families, friends, and neighbors in a spirit of friendly competition and community giving.

Each year, participants gather to compete in building innovative cardboard cat homes within a two-hour timeframe, organized by various CAT-egories that celebrate creativity, craftsmanship, and compassion. The event fosters teamwork, imagination, and a shared commitment to making a difference in our community.

Originally supporting the SPCA, Cardboard for Kitty has expanded its impact in recent years. The fundraiser now proudly donates to RedHead Strays, a local organization dedicated to rescuing and rehoming stray cats, and First Steps Saint John, which supports mothers and babies in our community. This dual focus allows us to extend our mission of helping both paws and people, creating a broader positive impact throughout Saint John.

Contributing to an organization that has served the community for over 20 years, supporting healthy moms, healthy babies, and healthy futures through housing and support services.
